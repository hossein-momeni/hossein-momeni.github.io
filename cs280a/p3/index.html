Part 1.1 Convolutions from Scratch

Implemented 4-loop and 2-loop 2D convolution with zero padding, and compared to scipy.signal.convolve2d.

Justification: Zero padding ensured boundary consistency; comparison showed identical results but NumPy loops were slower than SciPy’s C-optimized function.

Part 1.2 Finite Difference Operator

Cameraman: partials (Ix, Iy), gradient magnitude, and binarized edge map with a qualitatively chosen threshold.

Justification: Threshold chosen to suppress noise while keeping the key structural edges (tripod, hat brim) visible.

Part 1.3 Derivative of Gaussian (DoG)

Gaussian pre-smoothing reduces noise; DoG (G * Dx/Dy) matches blur-then-differentiate.

Justification: The DoG yields smoother, more coherent edges than plain finite differences by attenuating noise before differentiation.

Part 2.1 Image “Sharpening” (Unsharp Mask)

High-frequency boost via img + amount · (img − G_σ * img). Shows Taj and another example, with varying amount.

Justification: Unsharp masking emphasizes edges by adding back lost high-frequency detail, improving clarity without altering flat regions.

Part 2.2 Hybrid Images

Low-pass ⊕ high-pass; one pair with FFT analysis; two additional pairs.

Justification: Close-up perception is dominated by the high-pass, while distant viewing reveals the low-pass; color in the high-pass strengthened the effect.

Part 2.3 Gaussian & Laplacian Stacks

Stacks (no downsampling). Visualized for the Oraple pair.

Justification: Stacks keep all images the same size, making it easier to combine Laplacian levels directly during blending.

Part 2.4 Multiresolution Blending (Oraple)

Stack-based Laplacian blending using a Gaussian mask stack. Vertical seam and irregular masks.

Justification: Gaussian blurring of the mask at each level spreads the seam gradually, producing smooth transitions between sources.
